How This Dashboard Works

Methodology, logic, and data sources behind each section

1Product Adoption Summary

The summary at the top is a plain-English narrative synthesized from the underlying metrics. It's designed to give a CSM the full picture in 15 seconds. Here's how each statement is derived:

Engagement assessment — driven by Usage Levels of critical apps on the highest-ACV BU. If the majority of critical apps on the largest BU are at High or Medium Use, engagement is "strong." If most are Low Use or Undeployed, it's "weak."
Undeployed risk callout — triggered when any critical app is past the 180-day grace period and still classified as Undeployed (Activated - No Use or Not In Use). The ACV-weighted contribution is calculated as:

BU Undeployed % x BU ACV / Total ACV

This surfaces which BU is driving the most dollar-weighted risk. In this example, ITSM's 33% undeployed on $2.8M ACV = $924K undeployed, which is 70% of the total $1.3M undeployed ACV.
Stalled adoption flag — triggered when a BU shows NLU below 50% AND activity trend is flat or declining for 6+ consecutive months. Both conditions must be true — low NLU alone could mean a new BU still ramping.
Bright spot identification — any BU with 0% undeployed, NLU above 80%, and an upward activity trend is called out as a positive signal.
Health color (amber/red/green) — determined by the worst signal across the three KPIs:
• Green: Undeployed < 20%, NLU > 75%, no critical apps undeployed past grace
• Amber: Undeployed 20-50% OR NLU 50-75% OR 1-3 critical apps undeployed
• Red: Undeployed > 50% OR NLU < 50% OR 4+ critical apps undeployed

2Where to Focus (Prioritization Logic)

Focus areas are ranked by a weighted priority score combining three factors:

Priority Score = (ACV Weight x 0.5) + (Critical App Flag x 0.3) + (Time Past Grace x 0.2)

ACV Weight: BU ACV as a % of total account ACV. Higher-value BUs rank higher.
Critical App Flag: 1 if the app is in the 53 critical apps list, 0 otherwise.
Time Past Grace: Months past the 180-day grace period, normalized. Longer-neglected apps rank higher.

The "why it matters" narrative on each card is derived from:

Deployment status — Is the plugin activated? Is there any raw signal in the Customer Usage Dashboard?
BU context — Are sibling apps in the same BU deployed? (e.g., Discovery is High Use but Event Management is undeployed = likely integration blocker, not awareness issue)
NLU cross-reference — If BU NLU is also low, the issue is broader than one app (entire BU stalled vs. single app missed)

4Signal Definitions

Signals on the BU table and Application table follow these rules:

SignalCriteria
FocusCritical app is Undeployed (Activated - No Use or Not In Use) and past 180-day grace. OR: BU has NLU < 50% with flat/declining activity for 6+ months.
WatchApp is at Low Use, or NLU meter is between 50-70%, or activity trend is declining but not yet flat for 6 months.
On TrackApp is at Medium or High Use, NLU > 70%, activity trend stable or growing.

5Usage Level Badges

Each application is assigned a Usage Level based on BU-defined definitions. These definitions set thresholds against telemetry data (e.g., transactions closed, records created). Some definitions normalize for customer size, others use absolute thresholds — always check the specific definition.

6Utilization Table

The Utilization section offers two views:

By BU: Grouped by BU Workflow (e.g., Technology Workflow contains ITSM + ITOM). Shows Net Value (ACV), % Net License utilization, % Underutilization (negative = unused capacity), and % Overutilization (positive = over-consumption). Expandable groups let you drill from workflow to individual BU.
By SKU: Shows each individual product/SKU purchased by the customer, grouped by BU Workflow (collapsible). Columns: Product, Purchase Date, Product ACV (what was purchased), Usage ACV (what is being consumed), % NLU, % Under Utilization (red = unused capacity), and % Over Utilization (blue = over-consumption). NLU can exceed 100% when more licenses are consumed than purchased. SKUs with $0 ACV indicate entitled but unactivated products.

7Known Limitations

On-premises customers: No telemetry data available. Shows as "Not Measurable."
Regulated markets: US-based (including GCC) are measurable. Non-US regulated are currently not.
Multi-instance: Usage reflects the highest-activity instance at account level, not an aggregate.
Size normalization: Varies by application — some definitions adjust for customer size, others use absolute thresholds.
Pre-fix / Post-fix: NLU may not appear for subset BUs when licenses were sold as a bundled SKU. The coverage exists but isn't always visible at the subset level.
Trend data: Up to 18 months of monthly trend data available in PowerBI.

8Data Sources & Resources
